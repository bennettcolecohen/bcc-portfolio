Wo[R]d[L]e
Cracking viral game Wordle w/ Deep Reinforcement Learning

This project is being completed as part of my thesis/capstone for my M.S. at UC Berkeley. The goal of Wo(R)d(L)e is to build increasingly more robust algorithms to solve Wordle games to both minimize the average score and the amount of human information/intuition required. My early attempts use heuristic iterations based off of RegEx patterns and available words. The goal is now to implement more complex dynamic programming approaches until I arrive at a reinforcement learning solution that doesn’t require human knowledge of the English language. My inspiration for this project was reading the papers on AlphaGo and AlphaZero, the latter of which doesn’t use any human-expert knowledge to train, yet still outperforms the former 100-0 in test settings.
